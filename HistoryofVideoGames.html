History of Video Games

Video games are digital games that can be played on a computer device (e.g., tablets, smartphones, laptops, consoles, etc.). Originally designed simply for entertainment, video games have grown into their own industry and field of learning, generating billions of dollars each year.

The Rise of Video Games in the East (1985-1995)

Super Mario Brothers, and the Legend of Zelda were both released by Nintendo in 1985. Tetris was released for the Game Boy in 1989.

Tech Company Takeover (1995-2010)

In 1995 Sony, and Sega begin to compete with each other, with the Playstation (Sony) and Saturn System (Sega). While Nintendo released another console called the Nintendo 64.

Today in Gaming (2010-Now)

In 2005 and 2006, Microsoft released the Xbox 360, the same year, Sony released the Playstation 3, and also in the same year Nintendo released its Wii console. In 2012, Rovio released the popular mobile game Angry Birds. In 2012, Nintendo released its Wii U. In 2017 Nintendo released its successer the Nintendo Switch.
